Section 1.2 Quantitative Information 2

Subsection 1.2.1 Overview

This activity is designed to support work done in class on rates, ratios, and proportions.

Subsection 1.2.2 Review

A fraction is a way of representing parts of a whole number. Fractions take the form \(\dfrac{a}{b}\text{.}\) The top of a fraction is called the numerator and the bottom is called the denominator. The denominator tells us how many parts we break the whole number into, and the numerator tells us how many parts we have. Take the fraction \(\dfrac{5}{12}\) as an example. The fraction says that we are splitting a whole number into 12 total pieces, but we only have 5 of those pieces.

There are two kinds of fractions we'll deal with: proper fractions and improper fractions. A proper fraction is a fraction whose numerator is smaller than its denominator; \(\dfrac{5}{12}\) is an example of a proper fraction. An improper fraction is a fraction whose numerator is larger than the denominator; an example of an improper fraction is \(\dfrac{8}{3}\text{.}\)

Fractions are one way of representing numbers broken out into parts. Another way is by using decimals. A decimal is composed of two pieces: whole numbers greater than one (written on the left hand side of the decimal point) and numbers smaller than one (written to the right of the decimal point).

To write a fraction as a decimal, we perform division. For example, to convert the fraction \(\dfrac{2}{5}\) to a decimal, we do 2 divided by 5. Whether by long division or by calculator, we get the decimal value \(0.4\text{.}\)

Subsection 1.2.3 Problems

  1. Determine the ratio of first-semester students to non-first-semester students in the class.

  2. A small farm has 3 cows, 12 hens, 5 goats, and 7 roosters. Determine the ratio of cows to goats, hens to roosters, hens to goats, and cows to roosters.

  3. A vat has a salt concentration of 5 grams per liter. More brine is added to the mixture to raise the concentration to 9 grams per liter in 2 hours. At what rate did the concentration change?

  4. A recipe calls for a flour to fat ratio of 2.5:1. The thing you are baking currently has 4 cups of flour. How many cups of fat need to be added to follow the recipe?

  5. A 1473 class was polled about political alignment. It was determined that the ratio of Democrats to Independents was 3:2. If the ratio of Democrats to Republicans was 1:1, how many independents are in this class of 32 students?

  6. Solve the proportion for \(x\text{:}\) \(\dfrac{x}{10} = \dfrac{7.5}{17}\)
