A fraction is a way of representing parts of a whole number. Fractions take the form \(\dfrac{a}{b}\text{.}\) The top of a fraction is called the numerator and the bottom is called the denominator. The denominator tells us how many parts we break the whole number into, and the numerator tells us how many parts we have. Take the fraction \(\dfrac{5}{12}\) as an example. The fraction says that we are splitting a whole number into 12 total pieces, but we only have 5 of those pieces.
